# Storytelling Frameworks for Pitch Narration

Five battle-tested frameworks for structuring a pitch / demo / launch video script. Each
has a shape, an emotional arc, a best-fit profile, and a worked skeleton. In Phase 2, render
a **tailored 5–7 bullet outline per framework** using the real project, the Phase-1 stats,
and the user's demo sequence — then recommend the best fit (or a hybrid).

Across all five, hold the line on three disciplines:
- **Problem ≠ Impact.** The problem is the pain that motivates; the impact is what the work
  *unlocks* going forward. Never restate one as the other.
- **Tension before resolution.** Earn the solution by making the gap *felt* first.
- **One core message.** Every section advances a single arc toward one takeaway.

---

## Framework 1 — Problem → Solution (Classic Pitch)
**Shape:** Hook → Problem → Stakes → Solution → Demo → Impact → Close.
**Arc:** tension rising on the problem, released by the solution, lifted by the impact.
**Best for:** a clear, quantifiable pain point with a direct answer; technical-evaluator or
mixed audiences; the safe default when unsure.

**Skeleton**
- **Hook** — a sharp stat or scenario that makes the pain vivid in one breath.
- **Problem** — name the pain precisely; who feels it, how often, what it costs.
- **Stakes** — why it's getting worse / why now (a trend stat earns its place here).
- **Solution** — introduce the project as the answer; the one-line "what it is".
- **Demo** — show it working on the real flow (the wow moments land here).
- **Impact** — what this unlocks: extensibility, compounding ROI, industry consequence.
- **Close** — restate the core message + a forward call to action.

## Framework 2 — Hero's Journey (Transformation Arc)
**Shape:** Ordinary World → Call → Struggle → Guide/Tool → Transformation → New World.
**Arc:** a relatable protagonist (often "the user/developer") transformed by the product.
**Best for:** products that change *how someone works or feels*; emotionally resonant,
demo-day / investor / launch contexts; a strong single-user story.

**Skeleton**
- **Ordinary world** — a day in the life *before*; the quiet, accepted frustration.
- **The call** — the moment the pain becomes unbearable / the stakes spike (stat).
- **The struggle** — why existing tools/alternatives fail this hero (competitor gap).
- **The guide** — the project enters as the mentor/tool that changes the game.
- **Transformation (demo)** — watch the hero succeed with it, step by step.
- **New world** — life *after*; what's now possible that wasn't (impact, not problem).

---

## Framework 3 — Before → After → Bridge (BAB)
**Shape:** Before (pain world) → After (better world) → Bridge (how you get there).
**Arc:** contrast-driven; the gap between two worlds *is* the emotional engine.
**Best for:** a stark, visualizable before/after; time-limited pitches that need to land
fast; business/leadership audiences who buy outcomes.

**Skeleton**
- **Before** — paint the current painful reality concretely (anchor with a cost stat).
- **After** — paint the desired world vividly, *before* explaining how — let them want it.
- **Bridge** — the project is the bridge; show it (demo) carrying them across.
- **Proof** — the bridge holds: the demo's wow moment + one corroborating stat.
- **Impact** — having crossed, what new ground opens up.

---

## Framework 4 — SCQA (Situation → Complication → Question → Answer)
**Shape:** Situation (shared context) → Complication (what broke) → Question (the crux) →
Answer (your solution).
**Arc:** logical, consultative; builds inevitability so the answer feels *earned*.
**Best for:** analytical / technical / exec audiences; research-flavored pitches; when
credibility and rigor matter more than spectacle. (Also presentAS's deck spine — a natural
pairing when the same engagement needs both script and slides.)

**Skeleton**
- **Situation** — the stable, agreed-upon context everyone accepts.
- **Complication** — the disruption / gap that breaks the status quo (the key stat).
- **Question** — the sharp question the complication forces ("So how do we…?").
- **Answer** — the project as the answer; the demo proves it.
- **Implications** — what the answer makes possible at scale (impact).

---

## Framework 5 — What → Why → How (Capability-Led)
**Shape:** What it is → Why it matters → How it works → What's next.
**Arc:** clarity-first; orient the audience immediately, then deepen.
**Best for:** novel or hard-to-categorize products that need fast orientation; technical
peers/practitioners; when the *what* is genuinely new and confusion is the real risk.

**Skeleton**
- **What** — a crisp one-sentence definition of the thing (no jargon, no buildup).
- **Why** — the pain/opportunity that makes it matter (stat-backed tension).
- **How (demo)** — show the mechanism working on the real flow; the wow moment.
- **How (architecture, optional)** — the key components and *why* each was chosen.
- **What's next** — the roadmap/platform story: what this unlocks (impact).

---

## Choosing & hybridizing
- **Quantifiable pain, direct fix →** Problem→Solution or BAB.
- **Emotional, single-user transformation →** Hero's Journey.
- **Analytical / exec / research credibility →** SCQA.
- **Novel, confusing-at-first product →** What→Why→How.
- **Very tight time budget (< 2:00) →** BAB or Problem→Solution (least structural overhead).

**Hybrids that work well:** a Hero's-Journey *opening* (ordinary world) flowing into a
Problem→Solution *body*; or an SCQA *spine* with a BAB *close*. When recommending a hybrid,
name exactly which beats come from which framework and why.
